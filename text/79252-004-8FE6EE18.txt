Sadeq Hedayat, also spelled Ṣādeq-e Hedāyat or Sadiq Hidayat  (born February 17, 1903, Tehrān, Iran—died April 4, 1951, Paris, France) Iranian author who introduced modernist techniques into Persian fiction. He is considered one of the greatest Iranian writers of the 20th century.
Born into a prominent aristocratic family, Hedayat was educated first in Tehrān and then studied dentistry and engineering in France and Belgium. After coming into contact with the leading intellectual figures of Europe, Hedayat abandoned his studies for literature.
