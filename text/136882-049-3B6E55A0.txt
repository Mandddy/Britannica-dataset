Dame Barbara Hepworth, in full Dame Jocelyn Barbara Hepworth  (born Jan. 10, 1903, Wakefield, Yorkshire, Eng.â€”died May 20, 1975, St. Ives, Cornwall) sculptor whose works were among the earliest abstract sculptures produced in England. Her lyrical forms and feeling for material made her one of the most influential sculptors of the mid-20th century.
Fascinated from early childhood with natural forms and textures, Hepworth decided at age 15 to become a sculptor. In 1919 she enrolled in the Leeds School of Art, where she befriended fellow student Henry Moore. Their lifelong friendship and reciprocal influence were important factors in the parallel development of their careers.
