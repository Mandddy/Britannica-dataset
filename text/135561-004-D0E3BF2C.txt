Jonathan Dayton,  (born Oct. 16, 1760, Elizabethtown (now Elizabeth), N.J. [U.S.]â€”died Oct. 9, 1824, Elizabethtown, N.J., U.S.) youngest member of the U.S. Constitutional Convention, speaker of the U.S. House of Representatives, and developer of large tracts in what later became the state of Ohio. The city of Dayton, Ohio, is named for him.
Immediately following graduation from the College of New Jersey (Princeton University) in 1776, Dayton enlisted in the New Jersey militia. He fought in the New York and New Jersey campaigns, rose to the rank of captain, was at Yorktown in 1781, and returned to civilian life two years later.
