Jackie Gleason, original name Herbert John Gleason  (born February 26, 1916, Brooklyn, New York, U.S.â€”died June 24, 1987, Fort Lauderdale, Florida) American comedian best known for his portrayal of Ralph Kramden in the television series The Honeymooners.
Growing up in the slums of Brooklyn, Gleason frequently attended vaudeville shows, a habit that fueled his determination to have a stage career. His father abandoned the family in 1925, and in 1930 Gleason dropped out of high school in order to support his mother. He earned money with odd jobs, pool hustling, and performing in vaudeville. After the death of his mother in 1935, Gleason began to sharpen his comic talents in local nightclubs.
