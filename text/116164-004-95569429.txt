Fogg Art Museum,  museum founded at Harvard University in Cambridge, Mass., in 1895 as a study collection of Eastern and Western art from prehistory to the present, as well as an important art reference library of more than 150,000 volumes. The Fogg Museum is especially distinguished for its collection of drawings and prints.
Its research fields include classical art, coins, conservation, drawings, IslÄmic and Oriental art, photography, and prints. The Busch-Reisinger Museum at Harvard is a subsidiary.
