Willem Dafoe, original name William J. Dafoe  (born July 22, 1955, Appleton, Wisconsin, U.S.) American actor known for his versatility and willingness to appear in controversial roles.
Dafoe, the son of a surgeon and a nurse, was one of seven children. He studied theatre at the University of Wisconsin but left school to join Theater X, an experimental Wisconsin-based theatre troupe with which Dafoe toured for four years. In 1977 he moved to New York City and joined the Performance Group. There he met the actress and director Elizabeth LeCompte, who founded the deconstructionist theatre company the Wooster Group, with which Dafoe later performed.
