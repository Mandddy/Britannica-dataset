Saint Pius X, original name Giuseppe Melchiorre Sarto  (born June 2, 1835, Riese, Venetia, Austrian Empire [now in Italy]â€”died August 20, 1914, Rome, Italy; canonized May 29, 1954; feast day August 21) Italian pope from 1903 to 1914, whose staunch political and religious conservatism dominated the early 20th-century church.
Ordained in 1858, he became a parish priest in the Italian region of Venetia. Pope Leo XIII made him bishop of Mantua (1884) and in 1893 cardinal and patriarch of Venice. He was elected pope on August 4, 1903.
