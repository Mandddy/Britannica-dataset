Alberta Hunter,  (born April 1, 1895, Memphis, Tennessee, U.S.—died October 17, 1984, New York, New York) American blues singer who achieved international fame in the 1930s for her vigorous and rhythmically infectious style.
Hunter’s father abandoned the family soon after her birth. Her mother, who worked as a domestic in a brothel, remarried about 1906, but Alberta did not get along with her new family.
