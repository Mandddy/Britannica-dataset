Angela Lansbury,  (born October 16, 1925, London, England) British-born American character actress who achieved success and acclaim for her stage, film, and television work.
Lansbury and her widowed mother, actress Moyna MacGill, emigrated from England to the United States in 1940. From 1940 to 1942 Lansbury studied acting at the Feagin School of Drama and Radio in New York City. Her film debut came in the psychological thriller Gaslight (1944), and her performance as a devious Cockney maid earned her an Academy Award nomination for best supporting actress.
