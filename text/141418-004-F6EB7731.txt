Michel Rocard, in full Michel Louis Leon Rocard  (born August 23, 1930, Courbevoie, Franceâ€”died July 2, 2016, Paris) French public servant and politician who was premier of France from 1988 to 1991.
Upon graduating from the elite National School of Administration, Rocard became an inspector of finances in 1958 and subsequently rose to high posts in the government accounting service. He was early active in socialist politics, and in 1967 he became head of the small Unified Socialist Party, running unsuccessfully for the French presidency in 1969. He served in the National Assembly from 1969 to 1973 and from 1978 to 1981.
