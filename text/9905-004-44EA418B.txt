Sir Roderick Impey Murchison,  (born Feb. 19, 1792, Tarradale, Ross-shire, Scot.â€”died  Oct. 22, 1871, London, Eng.) geologist who first established the geologic sequence of Early Paleozoic strata (the Paleozoic Era began 542 million years ago and ended about 251 million years ago).
Murchison joined the Geological Society of London in 1825 and in the following five years explored Scotland, France, and the Alps and collaborated alternately with the British geologists Adam Sedgwick and Charles Lyell.
