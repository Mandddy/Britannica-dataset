Betty Carter, original name Lillie Mae Jones, also called Lorraine Carter or Lorene Carter  (born May 16, 1930, Flint, Michigan, U.S.â€”died September 26, 1998, Brooklyn, New York) American jazz singer who is best remembered for the scat and other complex musical interpretations that showcased her remarkable vocal flexibility and musical imagination.
Carter studied piano at the Detroit Conservatory of Music in her native Michigan. At age 16 she began singing in Detroit jazz clubs, and after 1946 she worked in black bars and theatres in the Midwest, at first under the name Lorene Carter.
