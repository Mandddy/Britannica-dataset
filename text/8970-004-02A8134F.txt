William Trevor, original name William Trevor Cox   (born May 24, 1928, Mitchelstown, County Cork, Ire.) Irish writer who is noted for his wry and often macabre short stories and novels.
Trevor was educated at Trinity College, Dublin, and began his career as a teacher and sculptor. He taught both history and art at various schools in Northern Ireland and England, and then in 1960 he moved to London and worked as an advertising copywriter.
