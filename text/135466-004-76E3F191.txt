Francis Burton Harrison,  (born Dec. 18, 1873, New York City—died  Nov. 21, 1957, Flemington, N.J., U.S.) U.S. governor general of the Philippines (1913–21) and later adviser to Philippine presidents.
Harrison was born into a wealthy and prominent family; his father was a successful New York lawyer who had been a private secretary to Jefferson Davis, and his mother was a well-known Virginia-born novelist. After receiving an A.B. from Yale University (1895) and a law degree from New York Law School (1897), he taught law, served in the Spanish–American War, and was elected to Congress as a Democrat, serving from 1903 to 1905 and from 1907 to 1912. The Harrison Narcotics Act (1914), which he espoused, laid the basis for the later Pure Food and Drug Act.
