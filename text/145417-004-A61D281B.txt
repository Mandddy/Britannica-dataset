Plane,  in carpentry, tool made in a wide variety of sizes, used for removing rough surfaces on wood and for reducing it to size. Many European guild craftsmen of the Middle Ages worked with beautifully decorated metal planes. Planes today are mostly machine-made, of wood and steel. Plow, or grooving, planes are used for forming channels or grooves; a wide variety of special models are employed for running moldings.
