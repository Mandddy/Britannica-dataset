Comcast, in full Comcast Corporation, formerly (1963â€“69) American Cable Systems,  major American provider of cable television, entertainment, and communications products and services. Its headquarters are in Philadelphia, Pa.
Comcast was founded in 1963 by Ralph J. Roberts, Daniel Aaron, and Julian A. Brodsky as a small cable system in Tupelo, Miss. In 1969 the company moved to Philadelphia and was renamed Comcast Corporation. Its initial growth was fueled by strategic investments in rival cable systems, including Group W Cable in 1986 and Storer Communications in 1988. Additional rivals were acquired, including E.W. Scripps in 1995 and Jones Intercable, Inc., in 1999. In 2002 Comcast purchased AT&amp;T Broadband, at the time the largest cable television operator in the United States, for $44.5 billion.
