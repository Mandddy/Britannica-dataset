Faith Hill, originally Audrey Faith Perry  (born Sept. 21, 1967, Jackson, Miss., U.S.) American country music singer known for her commercial success on both the country and pop music charts.
Hill grew up in Star, Miss., where she began singing at an early age. Her first public performance was at a 4-H luncheon at age 7. Influenced by Elvis Presley, Reba McEntire, and Tammy Wynette, Hill formed a band that played at local rodeos while in high school. At 19 she dropped out of college and moved to Nashville, Tenn., to pursue a career as a professional singer.
